Feijoada

Brazil's national dish, the name 'feijoada' comes from 'feijão', wich is bean in portuguese. The basic ingredients are fresh pork and black beans, it has many variations when it comes to other ingredients.

Coxinha

A crispy croquette filled with chicken meat and cream cheese, cleverly shaped into a chicken drumstick, then breaded and deep-fried. The name means "little thigh" and is how chicken legs are informally named in Brazil.

Caipirinha

Brazil's national cocktail, made with 'cachaça' (sugarcane hard liquor), sugar, and lime. The drink is prepared by mixing the fruit and the sugar together, then adding the liquor.

Brigadeiro

A brigadeiro is a brazilian dessert, generally shaped into small balls covered in chocolate sprinkles and placed in a small cupcake liner. The mixture may also be poured into a small container or eaten with a spoon.
